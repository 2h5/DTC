Industrial Controls and Automation

Posted January 10, 2022

Industrial Controls: History dates back to the times of Industrialization when a lot of improvements were made in the very domain. From that time, the practice continued. It has now evolved into industrial control systems and automation. This technically means the growing use of automatic systems to eliminate the human errors. These are mainly the devices that replace the manual labor force.

The modern world has an emphasis on modern solutions. Moreover, industries now are emerging with the best practices ever before. For example, when we talk about history, we find the legacy of General Electric’s GE Industrial Controls. Direct Turbine Controls is an online one-stop solution for people in dire need of repair of their industrial controls. We deal in testing and repair, new and refurbished circuit boards, training and customer service, and asset recovery. Engineers who practice controls and automation engineering are specialized to resolve issues regarding automatic controls. We have a huge team of authentic and experienced engineers working to deliver the best of their services.

Types of Automatic Controls

Industrial controls and automation comprises different components that are actuators, sensors, reference input, and the system. For the system to be automated, the need for actuators and sensors is mandatory. Furthermore, there are different types as well that need to be discussed to get a better picture of this enormous domain. These types are Electrical, Mechanical, and Electro-Mechanical.

�?       Electrical Control System

This type uses electrical energy as a source to operate the integrated machines. Hardware is closely connected with its components and input is transferred by sensors to the main circuit board with the help of electricity.

�?       Mechanical Control System

The mechanical control system is the practical and most technical type here. As it is practiced in emergencies like fire control systems, acoustics, or any building with mechanical machines. For instance, the appliances in our daily lives and the machines we use like air-conditioners, refrigerators, elevators, escalators, etc.

�?       Electro-Mechanical Control System

It is the most advanced type of automatic control as it is based on multiple relays, timers, and counters. A submerge of the Electrical and Mechanical system, this type is more complex and more essential. Examples of this system can be an electric motor and electric devices like a typewriter which converts electrical energy and mechanical efforts into a desirable output. This system typically uses every kind of relay and switch.
