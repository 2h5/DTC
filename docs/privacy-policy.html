Privacy Policy

1. Scope

This Policy describes how Direct Turbine Controls Corp. (“DTC,” “we,” “us”) collects, uses, discloses and protects personal information provided through this website, DTC's services and customer communications. It does not replace the privacy notice of a linked third party or payment provider.

The website and services are intended for business customers and adults, not children under 18. DTC does not knowingly seek personal information from children. If you believe a child supplied information, contact DTC so the situation can be reviewed and, where appropriate, the information deleted.

2. Information DTC collects

Information you provide

DTC may receive your name, business, job contact details, billing or shipping address, part numbers, equipment information, support messages and transaction details when you contact DTC by phone or email, request a quote, place an order, ship an item for service or otherwise communicate with DTC. The contact form in this repository is currently inert and does not transmit its fields.

Website and device information

Hosting and content-delivery providers can receive ordinary network information such as IP address, browser/device type, requested page, referring page, and date/time when a browser requests the site or its assets. The current repository does not include a first-party analytics or advertising tracker.

Cart and future checkout information

The current cart saves a record named dtc.cart.v1 in browser local storage. It contains SKU and quantity only; it does not contain a name, address, price or payment credential.

If checkout is enabled later, PayPal will collect payment and identity information under its own practices. DTC's systems are intended to receive and retain the order contents, transaction identifiers and status, and limited payer, contact and shipping information PayPal returns for fulfillment, support, fraud prevention, refunds, accounting and legal obligations. Full card credentials should not pass through or be stored by DTC's site or checkout server.

3. How DTC uses information

DTC may use information to:

  • respond to inquiries and provide quotes, technical support and customer service;
  • confirm, process, fulfill, ship, reconcile, refund and document orders or services;
  • verify compatibility and order details, prevent fraud and protect the website, customers and DTC;
  • operate, troubleshoot and improve the website and services;
  • maintain business, tax, warranty and accounting records and comply with applicable legal obligations;
  • send promotional information where DTC has an appropriate basis and honor applicable opt-out rights.

DTC will not claim a use that the deployed system does not perform. Material new uses require an updated notice and any consent or other basis required by applicable law.

4. How DTC shares information

DTC may provide information to service providers that perform functions on its behalf, such as website hosting, business communications, payment processing, fraud controls, database/backup services, accounting, shipping, insurance and professional advice. They should receive only information reasonably needed for their role and be subject to appropriate contractual and security requirements.

PayPal is an independent controller of payment customer data. Its collection and use is governed by the PayPal Privacy Statement. PayPal's payment-processing terms ask merchants to tell customers about that independent role and link to its notice.

DTC may also disclose information when reasonably necessary to comply with law or legal process, investigate fraud or security events, protect rights or safety, complete a business transaction with appropriate safeguards, or with your direction. DTC's currently published policy states that it does not sell or rent personal information for another party's own marketing without explicit consent.

5. Local storage, cookies and external resources

You can remove the local cart by clearing this site's browser storage; doing so clears the saved cart. Disabling browser storage may prevent cart persistence.

This build requests web fonts from Fontshare, so a visitor's browser connects to Fontshare to retrieve those assets. The site is currently hosted as static files on GitHub Pages. Those providers may process network information under their own policies. DTC should evaluate self-hosting fonts and identify every deployed provider before checkout launch.

The PayPal Web SDK does not load while checkout mode is off or order creation is disabled. When both launch gates are enabled, the browser will connect to PayPal and PayPal may use cookies or similar technologies for checkout, security and fraud prevention under its own notice. DTC should not deploy analytics, advertising pixels or a consent mechanism without updating this Policy to match.

6. Retention and security

DTC retains information for the period reasonably needed for the purpose for which it was collected and for applicable warranty, accounting, tax, dispute, fraud-prevention and legal obligations. The exact retention schedule for the planned online order database has not yet been approved and must be documented here before checkout is activated.

DTC uses reasonable administrative, technical and physical safeguards appropriate to the information and service. Planned checkout controls include limiting collection, restricting access, encrypting sensitive data in transit and at rest, redacting logs, maintaining backups, monitoring payment state and responding to incidents. No internet transmission or storage system is completely secure, and this Policy does not guarantee absolute security.

7. Your choices and privacy requests

You can choose not to provide optional information, although DTC may be unable to quote, fulfill or support a request without necessary details. You can clear the cart from your browser and use unsubscribe instructions in applicable marketing messages.

Depending on where you live and how applicable law treats the information, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, information about disclosure, or an appeal, and to withdraw consent where processing relies on consent. Rights can have exceptions. Send a request using the contact details below; DTC may need to verify identity and authority before acting.

DTC's selected hosting, payment and service providers may process information in other jurisdictions. Before checkout launch, DTC must document the deployed data locations and any legally required transfer safeguards.

8. Changes and contact

DTC may update this Policy to reflect changed practices or requirements. The review date will be updated when a revised version is posted, and additional notice will be provided where required.

Privacy questions or requests can be sent to info@dtc247.com, by phone at 201-244-6477, or by mail to Direct Turbine Controls Corp., 88J Portland Ave, Bergenfield, NJ 07621, USA.
