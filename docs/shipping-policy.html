Not an approved online shipping policy

Online checkout is not active. Final destinations, charges, taxes/duties, ship-by representations, title/risk terms and remedies require company and legal approval before payment is enabled.

1. Current status

DTC currently confirms shipment details in a written quote, order acknowledgement or invoice. Those transaction-specific documents control. The cart scaffold displays an item subtotal and does not implement an approved calculation for shipping or tax.

Do not interpret “in stock,” a service estimate, or a carrier estimate as a final ship or delivery commitment unless DTC confirms it for the order in writing.

2. Existing service information

DTC's existing FAQ says orders are normally shipped with UPS for domestic destinations and DHL for international destinations unless another carrier is requested when the order is placed. It also says actual service-level charges, handling and insurance for replacement value are included in shipping charges on the invoice.

This describes DTC's existing quote/invoice workflow. It does not decide how a future online checkout will calculate, collect or disclose those amounts, and it is not a guarantee that a carrier or service is available for every item or destination.

3. Decisions required before checkout launch

An authorized DTC owner and qualified advisers must approve and publish:

  • eligible domestic and international destinations, prohibited destinations, export-control/sanctions checks and any customer eligibility limits;
  • supported carriers and service levels, pickup options, hazardous/oversize restrictions, account-number use and expedited handling;
  • whether shipping, handling and insurance are charged during checkout or confirmed before payment, and how the buyer sees the complete amount before purchase;
  • sales/use tax, import duties, brokerage, customs documents, importer-of-record responsibilities and currency handling;
  • when title and risk of loss transfer, required signature, replacement-value insurance and claim authority;
  • the source and legal status of stock, ship-by and delivery estimates; cutoff times and business-day/holiday treatment;
  • address validation/change controls and treatment of failed delivery, refusal, unclaimed freight, rerouting and return-to-sender;
  • damage, shortage, wrong item, loss, delay, cancellation and refund procedures, evidence requirements and notice channels.

4. Controls and records to implement

The order record should preserve the customer-approved address, service level, charges, applicable policy version and promised ship-by basis. Address changes after payment need verified authorization and an audit trail. Fulfillment must be released only after payment is durably confirmed and inventory/condition checks pass.

DTC must monitor paid orders against their ship-by commitments. If a shipment cannot be made as represented, operations must provide a timely delay/cancellation/refund process that follows the approved policy and applicable law. Carrier status alone is not a substitute for customer communication or DTC's own records.

Tracking messages should reveal only what the intended recipient needs. Lost/damaged shipment and insurance claims should remain linked to the original order and any replacement/refund action so the customer is not charged twice.

5. Contact

For shipping on a current quote or order, email info@dtc247.com or call 201-244-6477 with the order reference, part number and destination.
